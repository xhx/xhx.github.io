</details>

### Problem 5
*Tags: First Pass 路 source: AMC10 2020 #5*

A fair coin is flipped 3 times. What is the probability of getting exactly 2 heads?

A) $\frac{1}{8}$  
B) $\frac{1}{4}$  
C) $\frac{3}{8}$  
D) $\frac{1}{2}$  
E) $\frac{3}{4}$

<details><summary>Strategic Analysis</summary>
<p><strong>First Pass:</strong> Problem #5, manageable. There are $2^3 = 8$ total outcomes. Getting exactly 2 heads can happen in $\binom{3}{2} = 3$ ways: HHT, HTH, THH. So the probability is $\frac{3}{8}$.</p>
<p><strong>Time Target:</strong> 1 minute</p>
</details>

<details><summary>Answer & Solution</summary>
<p><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<p>There are $2^3 = 8$ total outcomes. Getting exactly 2 heads can happen in $\binom{3}{2} = 3$ ways: HHT, HTH, THH. So the probability is $\frac{3}{8}$.</p>
</details>

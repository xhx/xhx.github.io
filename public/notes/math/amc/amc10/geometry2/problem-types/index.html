# Problem Type Key Strategy Difficulty
1 Hidden 30-60-90 and 45-45-90 triangles Recognize special right-triangle ratios to finish in one step 🟢 Easy
2 Pythagorean-triple perimeter/area Spot integer triples (3-4-5, 5-12-13, 8-15-17, …) embedded in diagrams 🟢 Easy
3 Altitude drops ⇒ similar triangles Use AA similarity from altitude or angle bisector in right/acute triangles 🟡 Medium
4 Inradius of a right triangle Apply $r=\dfrac{a+b-c}{2}$ or $A=rs$ to find radius or side 🟡 Medium
5 Circumradius with Extended Law of Sines Use $R=\dfrac{a}{2\sin A}$ (often with 60° or 120° angles) 🟡 Medium
6 Power of a Point (tangent–secant / chord–chord) Set up $PT^{2}=PA\!\cdot\!PB$ or $AE\!\cdot\!EC=BE\!\cdot\!ED$ 🟡 Medium
7 Cyclic quadrilateral angle chase Opposite angles sum to 180°; inscribed-angle theorem gives the rest 🟡 Medium
8 Mid-segment in trapezoid/triangle Midpoints create parallel lines; mid-segment length is average of bases 🟢 Easy
9 Area by Shoelace on lattice vertices Quick determinant gives half-integer area of polygon in grid 🟡 Medium
10 Coordinate-bash distance or slope Place key points at convenient coordinates; use distance/slope formulas 🟡 Medium
11 Similar polygons via parallel lines Parallel lines induce similar triangles; solve for lengths or ratios 🟡 Medium
12 Angle-bisector theorem ratio Internal or external bisector splits opposite side proportionally 🟡 Medium
13 Cevians concurrency (Ceva / mass points) Ratios on sides imply concurrency or find segment lengths 🔴 Hard
14 Circle tangent to two lines & a circle Center lies on angle bisector; set up right triangles for radius/coordinates 🔴 Hard
15 Three-dimensional box diagonal / face diagonal Compute $d=\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}+c^{2}}$ or $\sqrt{a^{2}+b^{2}}$ after unfolding 🟡 Medium
16 Cross-section of cube/prism Slice produces familiar 2-D shape; use similar triangles for area/perimeter 🟡 Medium
17 Cyclic ratio problems with Ptolemy Use $AC\!\cdot\!BD = AB\!\cdot\!CD + BC\!\cdot\!DA$ to solve for unknowns 🔴 Hard
18 Nested squares/triangles (infinite geometric area) Recurring similarity yields geometric series for total area or perimeter 🔴 Hard
19 Rotation/spiral similarity "pinwheel" Two equal-length chords imply 90° rotation or spiral similarity point 🔴 Hard
20 Geometric probability (dartboard regions) Ratio of areas/lengths in circle, square, or triangle gives probability 🟡 Medium

🎯 How to Use This Guide#

  1. Start with Easy Problems - Master the 🟢 Easy problems first (types 1, 2, 8)
  2. Progress to Medium - Work through 🟡 Medium difficulty problems (types 3-7, 9-12, 15-16, 20)
  3. Tackle Hard Problems - Challenge yourself with 🔴 Hard problems (types 13-14, 17-19)
  4. Practice Regularly - Solve problems from each category to build confidence

Number Theory Problems#

Practice Problems by Topic#

Divisibility and Primes#

Problem 1: Find the largest 4-digit number divisible by 36.

Problem 2: How many numbers from 1 to 1000 are divisible by 2, 3, or 5?

Problem 3: Find all digits $d$ such that $12d34$ is divisible by 11.

GCD and LCM#

Problem 4: Find $\gcd(123456, 789012)$.

Problem 5: Find all positive integer solutions to $15x + 25y = 100$.

Problem 6: Find the smallest positive integer $x$ such that $15x \equiv 1 \pmod{26}$.

Modular Arithmetic#

Problem 7: Find the last two digits of $7^{100}$.

Problem 8: Find $3^{1000} \bmod 1000$.

Problem 9: Solve $12x \equiv 8 \pmod{20}$.

Chinese Remainder Theorem#

Problem 10: Find the smallest positive integer $n$ such that:

  • $n \equiv 2 \pmod{3}$
  • $n \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$
  • $n \equiv 1 \pmod{5}$
  • $n > 100$

Sequences and Series#

Problem 11: Find the 100th term of the arithmetic sequence 2, 5, 8, 11, …

Problem 12: Find the sum of the first 50 terms of the geometric sequence 3, 6, 12, 24, …

Problem 13: Find the 20th Fibonacci number.

Advanced Topics#

Problem 14: Find the smallest positive integer $n$ such that $n!$ is divisible by $2^{100}$.

Problem 15: Find all positive integer solutions to $x^2 + y^2 = 25$.

Problem 16: Find the largest integer that cannot be expressed as $7x + 11y$ for non-negative integers $x$ and $y$.

Solution 2#

Use inclusion-exclusion principle:

  • Divisible by 2: 500 numbers
  • Divisible by 3: 333 numbers
  • Divisible by 5: 200 numbers
  • Divisible by 6: 166 numbers
  • Divisible by 10: 100 numbers
  • Divisible by 15: 66 numbers
  • Divisible by 30: 33 numbers

Answer: 500 + 333 + 200 - 166 - 100 - 66 + 33 = 734

Solution 3#

Alternating sum: 1 - 2 + d - 3 + 4 = d For divisibility by 11: d = 0

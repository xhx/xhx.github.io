üéØ What is the Euclidean Algorithm?#

The Euclidean algorithm is an efficient method for finding the greatest common divisor (GCD) of two integers. It’s based on the principle that $\gcd(a,b) = \gcd(b, a \bmod b)$.

üîç The Algorithm#

Basic Steps#

  1. Start with two positive integers $a$ and $b$ where $a \geq b$
  2. Apply the recurrence relation: $\gcd(a,b) = \gcd(b, a \bmod b)$
  3. Continue until one of the numbers becomes 0
  4. The non-zero number is the GCD

Example: Find $\gcd(48, 18)$#

Step 1: gcd(48, 18) = gcd(18, 48 mod 18) = gcd(18, 12)
Step 2: gcd(18, 12) = gcd(12, 18 mod 12) = gcd(12, 6)
Step 3: gcd(12, 6) = gcd(6, 12 mod 6) = gcd(6, 0)
Step 4: gcd(6, 0) = 6

Answer: $\gcd(48, 18) = 6$

üìä Extended Euclidean Algorithm#

The extended Euclidean algorithm not only finds the GCD but also finds integers $x$ and $y$ such that $\gcd(a,b) = ax + by$ (B√©zout’s identity).

Example: Find $\gcd(48, 18)$ and express it as $48x + 18y$#

Step-by-step solution:

Step a b q r x y
1 48 18 2 12 1 -2
2 18 12 1 6 -1 3
3 12 6 2 0 2 -5
4 6 0 - - - -

Working backwards:

  • From step 3: $6 = 12 - 2 \cdot 6 = 12 - 2 \cdot (18 - 1 \cdot 12) = 3 \cdot 12 - 2 \cdot 18$
  • From step 1: $12 = 48 - 2 \cdot 18$
  • Substituting: $6 = 3 \cdot (48 - 2 \cdot 18) - 2 \cdot 18 = 3 \cdot 48 - 8 \cdot 18$

Answer: $\gcd(48, 18) = 6 = 3 \cdot 48 + (-8) \cdot 18$

üéØ Applications in AMC Problems#

1. Solving Linear Diophantine Equations#

Problem: Find all integer solutions to $48x + 18y = 6$

Solution:

  1. Check if $\gcd(48, 18) = 6$ divides 6 ‚úì
  2. Find particular solution: $x_0 = 3, y_0 = -8$
  3. General solution: $x = 3 + \frac{18}{6}t = 3 + 3t$, $y = -8 - \frac{48}{6}t = -8 - 8t$

2. Finding Modular Inverses#

Problem: Find the modular inverse of 18 modulo 48

Solution:

  1. Use extended Euclidean algorithm
  2. Find $x$ such that $18x \equiv 1 \pmod{48}$
  3. From above: $3 \cdot 48 + (-8) \cdot 18 = 6$
  4. Multiply by $\frac{1}{6}$: $\frac{1}{6} \cdot 48 + (-\frac{4}{3}) \cdot 18 = 1$
  5. Since $\gcd(18, 48) = 6 \neq 1$, no inverse exists

3. Simplifying Fractions#

Problem: Simplify $\frac{48}{18}$

Solution:

  1. Find $\gcd(48, 18) = 6$
  2. Divide both numerator and denominator by 6
  3. $\frac{48}{18} = \frac{8}{3}$

‚ö° Speed Techniques#

Quick Recognition#

  1. Consecutive integers: $\gcd(n, n+1) = 1$
  2. Even and odd: $\gcd(2n, 2n+1) = 1$
  3. Powers of 2: $\gcd(2^a, 2^b) = 2^{\min(a,b)}$
  4. Prime factors: If $p$ is prime, $\gcd(p, n) = 1$ or $p$

Mental Math Shortcuts#

  1. Small numbers: Use prime factorization
  2. Large numbers: Use Euclidean algorithm
  3. Patterns: Look for common factors first

üß† Common Problem Types#

Type 1: Find GCD of two numbers#

Strategy: Apply Euclidean algorithm directly

Example: Find $\gcd(123456, 789012)$ Solution:

gcd(123456, 789012) = gcd(789012, 123456) = gcd(123456, 789012 mod 123456)
= gcd(123456, 123456) = 123456

Type 2: Solve Diophantine equations#

Strategy: Use extended Euclidean algorithm

Example: Find all positive integer solutions to $15x + 25y = 100$ Solution:

  1. $\gcd(15, 25) = 5$ and $5 \mid 100$ ‚úì
  2. Particular solution: $x = 5, y = 1$
  3. General solution: $x = 5 + 5t, y = 1 - 3t$ for $t \geq 0$

Type 3: Find modular inverses#

Strategy: Use extended Euclidean algorithm

Example: Find $15^{-1} \pmod{26}$ Solution:

  1. $\gcd(15, 26) = 1$ ‚úì
  2. Extended algorithm gives: $1 = 7 \cdot 15 + (-4) \cdot 26$
  3. $15^{-1} \equiv 7 \pmod{26}$

üö® Common Mistakes to Avoid#

  1. Wrong order: Make sure $a \geq b$ initially
  2. Sign errors: Be careful with negative numbers
  3. Forgetting to check existence: For Diophantine equations, check if $\gcd(a,b) \mid c$
  4. Calculation errors: Double-check your arithmetic

üîó Related Concepts#

  • B√©zout’s Identity: $\gcd(a,b) = ax + by$ for some integers $x, y$
  • Linear Diophantine Equations: $ax + by = c$ has solutions iff $\gcd(a,b) \mid c$
  • Modular Inverses: $a^{-1} \pmod{m}$ exists iff $\gcd(a,m) = 1$
  • LCM Relationship: $\gcd(a,b) \cdot \text{lcm}(a,b) = ab$

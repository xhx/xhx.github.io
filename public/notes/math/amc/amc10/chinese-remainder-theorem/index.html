Chinese Remainder Theorem (CRT)#

The Chinese Remainder Theorem (CRT) is a powerful tool in number theory that lets you solve systems of modular congruences when the moduli are pairwise coprime.

Chinese Remainder Theorem (Statement)#

Let $m_1, m_2, \ldots, m_k$ be pairwise coprime positive integers (i.e., $\gcd(m_i, m_j) = 1$ for all $i \neq j$).

Let $a_1, a_2, \ldots, a_k$ be any integers.

Then the system of congruences:

$$\begin{cases} x \equiv a_1 \pmod{m_1} \ x \equiv a_2 \pmod{m_2} \ \vdots \ x \equiv a_k \pmod{m_k} \end{cases}$$

has a unique solution modulo $M = m_1 m_2 \cdots m_k$.

How to Construct the Solution#

Let’s solve:

$$x \equiv a_i \pmod{m_i} \quad \text{for } i = 1, 2, \ldots, k$$

Define:

  • $M = m_1 m_2 \cdots m_k$
  • $M_i = \frac{M}{m_i}$ for each $i$
  • Let $y_i = M_i^{-1} \pmod{m_i}$ (i.e., modular inverse of $M_i$ mod $m_i$)

Then:

$$x \equiv \sum_{i=1}^{k} a_i M_i y_i \pmod{M}$$

This gives the unique solution modulo $M$.

Example#

Solve:

$$\begin{cases} x \equiv 2 \pmod{3} \ x \equiv 3 \pmod{4} \ x \equiv 1 \pmod{5} \end{cases}$$

Step 1: Moduli: 3, 4, 5 â†’ pairwise coprime

So, $M = 3 \cdot 4 \cdot 5 = 60$

Step 2: Compute $M_i$

  • $M_1 = 60 / 3 = 20$
  • $M_2 = 60 / 4 = 15$
  • $M_3 = 60 / 5 = 12$

Step 3: Find inverses $y_i = M_i^{-1} \pmod{m_i}$

  • $y_1 = 20^{-1} \pmod{3} = 2$ (since $20 \cdot 2 = 40 \equiv 1 \pmod{3}$)
  • $y_2 = 15^{-1} \pmod{4} = 3$
  • $y_3 = 12^{-1} \pmod{5} = 3$

Step 4: Construct the solution:

$$x \equiv 2 \cdot 20 \cdot 2 + 3 \cdot 15 \cdot 3 + 1 \cdot 12 \cdot 3 \pmod{60}$$

$$x \equiv 80 + 135 + 36 = 251 \pmod{60} \Rightarrow x \equiv 11 \pmod{60}$$

Final Answer#

$$\boxed{x \equiv 11 \pmod{60}}$$

Applications in AMC Problems#

Finding Least Positive Integer#

Problem: Find the least positive integer $n$ such that $n \equiv 2 \pmod{3}$, $n \equiv 3 \pmod{4}$, $n \equiv 1 \pmod{5}$, and $n > 100$.

Solution: From above, $n \equiv 11 \pmod{60}$. The smallest solution greater than 100 is $n = 11 + 60 \cdot 2 = 131$.

Solving Systems of Congruences#

CRT is essential for problems involving multiple modular constraints, especially when finding specific values that satisfy multiple conditions.
